Local SEO

Your Google Business Profile is doing more work than your website

For a lot of local businesses, the Google Business Profile is the first — and sometimes only — thing a potential customer sees.

When someone searches for a business like yours nearby, the results they see first are often a map, a handful of star ratings, and a short list of details — all pulled from Google Business Profile. Your website might not appear until they scroll past that, or click through from it. If that profile is thin, outdated, or missing entirely, you're losing the first impression before a visitor ever reaches your homepage.

What a complete profile actually includes

A fully built-out profile goes well beyond a name, address and phone number. It includes accurate opening hours (including holiday hours), the right business category and any relevant secondary categories, a clear list of services or products, regularly added photos, and short posts about offers, events or updates. Google also lets people ask questions directly on your profile — and leaves those questions visible to everyone until someone answers them.

Reviews do more than build trust

Reviews are often treated as a vanity metric, but they influence both whether someone chooses you and how visible you are in local search results in the first place. A steady flow of recent reviews — and replies to them, good or bad — signals that a business is active and responsive. Ignoring reviews, especially negative ones, leaves an impression that nothing has changed since the complaint was posted.

The most common gaps we see

The same handful of issues show up again and again: hours that don't reflect a recent change, a photo gallery that hasn't been updated in years, unanswered questions sitting at the top of the profile, and duplicate listings created by accident over time that split reviews and confuse search engines. None of these are difficult to fix on their own — the challenge is usually finding the time to do it consistently.

A well-maintained Google Business Profile won't replace a good website, but it often does more of the day-to-day work of getting found, building trust, and turning a search into a visit or a call. If your profile hasn't had attention in a while, it's usually one of the fastest things to improve.
